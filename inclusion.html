Diversity in Digital

Diversity in Digital is a grassroots group of employees from across the organization who are passionate about helping the company achieve a more diverse and inclusive workforce. Their mission is to partner with leadership and employees to help the company enact its value of cultivating a diverse workforce, with focus on race, age, differently-abled, and LGBTQ identities and their intersections.

The three areas of focus for this task force are:
Increasing recruitment of diverse talent
Nurturing an inclusive environment that drives invention and retention
Ensuring career advancement opportunities for employees of all backgrounds




Unconscious Bias Training

The company is committed to being an inclusive and equitable place to work for every individual, so in 2017 Talent & Inclusion introduced unconscious bias training for all employees. Unconscious bias education helps us all recognize and ultimately minimize how biases may affect our decision-making. Paradigm, a team of experts in this area, facilitated several in-person trainings for our colleagues last year.

Microaggressions: A Few Examples

Of those who attended training...
99% of respondents understand the concept of unconscious bias.
86% of respondents thought the material was relevant to them.
97% of respondents intend to engage in behaviors to reduce bias.
90% of respondents would recommend the workshop to a colleague.


2018 Unconscious Bias Training Efforts
We're currently rolling out mandatory unconscious bias training to all hiring managers through an online vendor called Emtrain. The training was developed with Paradigm, the organization that facilitated our in-person training sessions. The course is 30 minutes long and covers the following learning objectives: Recruiting and Hiring, Team Dynamics, and Career Advancement.







Diversity Efforts in Hiring

When looking to fill our open positions, we consider a diverse candidate slate as well as ensure that our interview panels are diverse. This is our commitment." - Stacey Olive, VP Talent Acquisition

At The New York Times, building a talented and diverse workforce is a vital part of our mission. We are committed to a hiring process that is inclusive, fair and transparent, and one that puts emphasis on the company's commitment to career development. While every department is unique and has its own needs, this toolkit is intended to help toward those goals. We have identified four mandatory core principles that apply across the company. These four principles are universal requirements, and part of the full hiring process detailed here.

Post the job: All open positions must be posted. The few exceptions will require masthead- or E.V.P.-level approval. Accept applications in Workday: Serious candidates must submit their applications through Workday. This helps us track our recruiting efforts, which is a legal requirement and makes our work more effective. Seek a diverse pool of applicants: At a minimum, the qualified candidates we consider for every role should be gender diverse and include at least one person of color. Form a panel of interviewers from diverse backgrounds: The interviewers must also be ethnically and gender diverse.
