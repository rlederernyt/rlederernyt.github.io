Diversity Report

On March 28, 2018, The New York Times released its inaugural public report on diversity and inclusion. In this report, we provide an overview of our efforts to build a more diverse and inclusive culture at The Times. And, for the first time, we are sharing detailed data about the ethnic and gender composition of our staff members — data that we intend to update and make public annually.









Unconscious Bias Training

The New York Times is committed to being an inclusive and equitable place to work for every individual, so in 2017 Talent & Inclusion introduced unconscious bias training for all employees. Unconscious bias education helps us all recognize and ultimately minimize how biases may affect our decision-making. Paradigm, a team of experts in this area, facilitated several in-person training for our colleagues in 2016. These are the results of the survey we sent to all the workshop attendees:


* 99% of respondents understand the concept of unconscious bias.
* 86% of respondents thought the material was relevant to them.
* 97% of respondents intend to engage in behaviors to reduce bias.
* 90% of respondents would recommend the workshop to a colleague.


2018 Unconscious Bias Training Efforts
We're currently rolling out mandatory unconscious bias training to all hiring managers through an online vendor called Emtrain. The training was developed with Paradigm, the organization that facilitated our in-person training sessions. The course is 30 minutes long and covers the following learning objectives: Recruiting and Hiring, Team Dynamics, and Career Advancement.

Diversity Efforts in Hiring

At The New York Times, building a talented and diverse workforce is a vital part of our mission. We are committed to a hiring process that is inclusive, fair and transparent, and one that puts emphasis on the company's commitment to career development. While every department is unique and has its own needs, this toolkit is intended to help toward those goals. We have identified four mandatory core principles that apply across the company:

1. Post the job.
All open positions must be posted. The few exceptions will require masthead- or E.V.P.-level approval.
2. Accept applications in Workday.
 Serious candidates must submit their applications through Workday. This helps us track our recruiting efforts, which is a legal requirement and makes our work more effective.
3. Seek a diverse pool of applicants.
At a minimum, the qualified candidates we consider for every role should be gender diverse and include at least one person of color.
4. Form a panel of interviewers from diverse backgrounds.
The interviewers must also be ethnically and gender diverse.
