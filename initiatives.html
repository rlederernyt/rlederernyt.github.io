Unconscious Bias Training

The company is committed to being an inclusive and equitable place to work for every individual, so in 2017 Talent & Inclusion introduced unconscious bias training for all employees. Unconscious bias education helps us all recognize and ultimately minimize how biases may affect our decision-making. Paradigm, a team of experts in this area, facilitated several in-person trainings for our colleagues last year. The feedback was very positive and they were asked to offer more this year.

Microaggressions: A Few Examples

Of those who attended training...
99% of respondents understand the concept of unconscious bias.
86% of respondents thought the material was relevant to them.
97% of respondents intend to engage in behaviors to reduce bias.
90% of respondents would recommend the workshop to a colleague.




Diversity Efforts in Hiring

We have a number of initiatives to set up a talent pipeline, and recruit young people from underrepresented groups to work at the Times. We maintain partnerships with organizations like Code2040, the Emma Bowen Foundation, and the T Howard Foundation to recruit talent. We also have Times specifics programs to mentor and support young people.

New York Times Student Journalism Institute
For college students who are members of the National Association of Black Journalists or the National Association of Hispanic Journalists.
New York Times College Scholarship Program
For high school graduates. Recipients receive a partial four-year college scholarship, mentoring and a summer internship at The New York Times.

The Times is committed to hiring and retaining diverse employees across all departments. The technology department has laid out interview practices to reduce bias in hiring, and has also mandated that there be at least one female on every hiring panel.
