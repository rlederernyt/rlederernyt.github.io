Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) bring people together as a way to educate and promote an environment of understanding, acceptance and diversity and inclusion among all employees. Sign up here.





Arab Collective
The NYT Arab Collective brings together Company staff members of Arab descent and those with interest in the region to encourage deepened awareness and understanding of Arab culture, history and current events. For many Arab-Americans and Arabs who come to The Times from other communities where they have felt isolated, our ERG is a visible signal that they do not have to be alone. Especially at a time of heightened political tensions involving Arabs and Muslims, who are often identified with terrorism or subject to prejudice, our group can be a supportive force. Many Arab immigrants who have come to The Times have left behind societies in crisis, and struggle to come to terms with a sense of displacement. This group offers a solid foundation from which they can develop both professionally and personally. Our group serves as a resource for the newsroom and business side and aims to nourish a sense of community among members, offer mutual support, and organize cultural events and volunteer activities. Our involvement demonstrates the company’s commitment to a diverse array of people, experience and perspectives. We have five specific areas of focus: – Meet regularly to discuss current affairs and the challenges facing Arabs and Arab-Americans in today’s fraught political environment. – Organize events tied to Arab culture or themes in the news: These could include speakers, panel discussions moderated by a staff member, organized film viewings, etc. – Provide informal mentorship to young, developing journalists and business-side employees. – Attract talented candidates of Arab origin for job openings on the news and business sides. – Provide expertise through a Stylebook committee to improve the accuracy of language we use in the report and establish a public Slack channel to allow staffers to ask questions and share expertise.

Asian Network
The Asian Network advocates for the company’s diversity and inclusion goals, as well as for issues of interest to the Asian and Asian American community at The Times and at large. We are committed to being a resource, a gathering place for fellowship and fun and an amplified voice focused on diversity, career fulfillment and the company’s future.

Black@NYT
Black@NYT cultivates leadership and growth opportunities for Black employees and supports the Company’s inclusion objectives around the recruitment and retention of a diverse talent pool. Black@NYT is open to all employees and its members sponsor initiatives throughout the year including professional development, Company policy advocacy, arts and culture events, happy hours and volunteer opportunities.

Latino Network
We are dedicated to helping @nytimes recruit and retain Latino colleagues and are also active in engaging the #Latino community at large. The Latino Network will serve as a central resource for its members to increase cultural awareness, and access information on community outreach and professional development. By our actions, we seek to inspire commitment to a diversity of people, perspectives and voices that will help The New York Times become a more successful and innovative company.

Times Cares
Times Cares is a multi-generational group open to all employees of The New York Times Company. Our mission is to be a resource and advocate for work-related topics and life experiences that impact everyone. Using diverse work experiences, we seek to inform, educate, collaborate, debate and support each other in addition to increasing awareness of those Times programs and benefits available to all.

Times Out
Times Out, the GLBTQ network, helps to foster a fair and safe workplace at all NYTCo properties, educate NYTCo employees and senior management on issues related to our community, take a leadership role, as appropriate, with outside organizations that fit our mission, and serve as an in-house “focus group” (as appropriate, based on employees’ job responsibilities) on markets, products, recruitment, retention and other areas that help move our company forward.

Women's Network
The Women’s Network is dedicated to helping employees develop the skills and relationships necessary to advance their careers while maintaining a balance between their professional and personal lives. The Network supports employees trying to navigate a changing media environment and provides a forum for discussion about issues that matter to women.

Times Reads
The purpose of Times Reads is to help engage our ERG members in conversation on a topic that embraces our diversity.
