The Gender Project

A cross-departmental initiative to better engage women readers and infuse a gender perspective into our coverage at large. Led by Jessica Bennett. Launched in November; recent projects include Rites of Passage, Overlooked , 45 Stories of Sexual Consent on Campus, Gender Letter and NYT Gender on Instagram. Internally, you can join the conversation via #gender slack, or gender@nytimes.com.

Race/Related

Race/Related Meetings are weekly meetings that merge the Business and Newsroom to discuss news coverage as it relates to race and diversity. They allow employees around the company to openly convey emotions and thoughts regarding race within the news coverage and various perspectives that should be considered when covering specific news stories. The group sends out a newsletter "exploring race with provocative reporting and discussion."

Meetings every Tuesday, 11 a.m. 4th Floor PO-N-Large-04P2-320

“Marc [Lacey] has ably led our newly established team on race, collaborating with a range of desks and editors to tell the story of race and identity in new forms, including the Race/Related newsletter, the interactive on how views of the police shaped interpretations of body camera videos, and a stream of ambitious stories and social callouts.”
– Dean Baquet, Executive Editor

NYT Student Journalism Institute

The Institute is offered once a year in May. Participants must be students (or December or May graduates) who are members of the National Association of Hispanic Journalists or the National Association of Black Journalists or students at a historically black college or university. In a supportive environment of students and staff who share the same goals, aspiring journalists are given the opportunity to learn to produce material that meets the standards of the most prestigious and demanding news organizations in the nation.

"At my school, there’s not really space to network with other black journalists... it was cool to have a space where everyone is part of that community. You realize you're not alone in this feeling of uncomfortability or struggle... it helps you understand your place in the industry a little bit more and feel comfortable trying to navigate it, knowing that there are other people thinking about the exact same things. And it’s even cooler knowing that there are people who are staff here at the Times who are also thinking about it, and they’re willing to take the time to help bring us up the pipeline. It was a really positive experience."

– Tyler Blint-Welsh, NYTSJI Alum, Metro Intern
