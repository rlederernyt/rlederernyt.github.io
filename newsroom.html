The Gender Initiative

The New York Times Gender Initiative is a cross-platform, global offering to elevate and expand The Times’s coverage of gender issues and deepen our engagement with female readers around the globe. The way we see it, gender is a lens through which we view the world. The Gender Initiative comprises a dedicated team of of women at The New York Times focused on amplifying coverage, driving conversation, building a community, and understanding audience built around gender issues.

Gender Initiative Projects:
* Special coverage like 45 Stories of Sex and Consent on Campus, Overlooked, How #MeToo Changed Their Lives
* Live events like The New Rules Summit
* Consistent updates via the gender newsletter and @nytgender on Instagram
* Launching new products like Rites of Passage (a new column in Styles focused on personal narrative)
* A series of guides focused on Women and Work with Smarter Living, and more.
* Internal research. See: Understanding our Female Audience
* Working more broadly to change women’s perception of The Times as “not for them.”
Gender Initiative Tools:
* Gender analysis of bylines on page A1 of print
 * Female Readers Bot (Blue Kai): Shows stories over-indexing (for the desk) with female readers
* Analysis of Times Tags: Shows percentages of articles about men vs. women by desk
* Honorifics & Pronouns: Chrome extension tool to count honorifics and pronouns in stories
* Gender Meme: Tool to count gender of sources and people in an article

Race/Related

Race/Related Meetings are weekly meetings that merge the Business and Newsroom to discuss news coverage as it relates to race and diversity. They allow employees around the company to openly convey emotions and thoughts regarding race within the news coverage and various perspectives that should be considered when covering specific news stories. The group sends out a newsletter "exploring race with provocative reporting and discussion."

Marc [Lacey] has ably led our newly established team on race, collaborating with a range of desks and editors to tell the story of race and identity in new forms, including the Race/Related newsletter, the interactive on how views of the police shaped interpretations of body camera videos, and a stream of ambitious stories and social callouts.”

– Dean Baquet, Executive Editor
