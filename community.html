Employee Resource Groups


Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) bring people together as a way to educate and promote an environment of understanding, acceptance and diversity and inclusion among all employees. Sign up here.


Arab Collective
Brings together Company staff members of Arab descent and those with interest in the region; Involvement demonstrates the company’s commitment to a diverse array of people, experience and perspectives.

Asian Network
The Asian Network advocates for the company’s diversity and inclusion goals, as well as for issues of interest to the Asian and Asian American community at The Times and at large.

Black@NYT
Black@NYT cultivates leadership and growth opportunities for Black employees and supports the Company’s inclusion objectives around the recruitment and retention of a diverse talent pool; Active on diversity, recruitment, career development. Mark Thompson is executive sponsor.

Latino Network
Dedicated to helping the New York Times recruit and retain Latino colleagues and are also active in engaging the Latino community at large.

Times Cares
Multi-generational group open to all employees of The New York Times Company; Seek to inform, educate, collaborate, debate, and support each other in addition to increasing awareness using diverse work experiences

Times Out
GLBTQ network; Serve as an in-house "focus group" (as appropiate, based on employees' job responsibilities) on markets, products, recruitment, retention, and other areas that help move our Company forward.

Women's Network
Supports employees trying to navigate a changing media environment and provides a forum for discussion about issues that matter to women.

Times Reads
Engages our ERG members in conversation on a topic that embraces our diversity.




Diversity In Digital

Diversity in Digital is a grassroots group of employees from across the organization who are passionate about helping the company achieve a more diverse and inclusive workforce. Their mission is to partner with leadership and employees to help the company enact its value of cultivating a diverse workforce, with focus on race, age, differently-abled, and LGBTQ identities and their intersections.

The three areas of focus for this task force are:
Increasing recruitment of diverse talent
Nurturing an inclusive environment that drives invention and retention
Ensuring career advancement opportunities for employees of all backgrounds

The 10% Commitment Agreement is an agreement that employees in Chief Technology Officer Nick Rockwell's reporting structure who participate in the Diversity in Digital task force are permitted to spend up to 10% of their working hours each week devoted to Diversity in Digital initiatives provided that the participant expectations (as outlined in the document) are met. Find out more about Diversity in Digital’s initiatives here.

“It has made me feel more ownership. It’s empowered me to think about ways to make change.”

- Stephanie Kim, Manager of Customer Experience, Mobile Product

“Being involved in groups that advocate for higher diversity and more transparency on our efforts as an organization on this topic [diversity] meant that I can meet people and work with people who are more relentless with desiring change and taking actions. I think that’s the benefit with me personally is to be able to develop allies with these type of people.”

- Robin Lee, Senior Analyst, Subscriber Reporting and Analytics

“I feel engaged. I feel accepted also because I can really come to this group or other things that I’m part of and I’m just accepted as a project manager, as a person who is interested in these things. I get to be opinionated and colorful.”

- Leza Zaman, Project Manager


Women In Tech

Women in Tech is a team that empowers to create and drive initiatives that will increase the number of women in our technology organization, focusing on: recruiting and hiring high quality talent, with an emphasis on identifying women in technology; retaining, supporting and promoting an environment where female talent thrives; and strengthening the internal pipeline of women into leadership positions.
